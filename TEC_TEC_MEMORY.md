## COMPETITOR INTELLIGENCE

*Updated: 2026-05-04 — Mission 1 complete. Full profiles in COMPETITORS.md.*

**5-line summary:**
1. The Mauritius pharmacy SPF market is dominated by La Roche-Posay (Rs 820–1,550, chemical and mineral, marine-tested but reef-safe is not a brand pillar) with no cause narrative and no local identity.
2. Nivea and Garnier own the supermarket mass-market at Rs 280–600 estimated but are chemical-filter brands with no reef-safe conviction and no Mauritius relevance.
3. The specialist reef-safe mineral brands (Stream2Sea, Thinksport, EQ Love) have zero Mauritius shelf presence — the position is entirely unoccupied in-market.
4. No sunscreen brand — anywhere in the competitive set — links purchase to a named Mauritian skin cancer cause. This is the sharpest white space.
5. Pradiance and Maxiblock confirm that mineral sunscreen can physically reach Mauritius shelves (pharmacy and eco-online) but neither has meaningful brand presence, story, or cause — the mineral slot in the pharmacy channel is available.

**Key price anchors for Phase 3 pricing:**
- Mass supermarket: Rs 280–600 (Nivea/Garnier est.)
- Mid pharmacy: Rs 820–1,200 (La Roche-Posay entry, Avène)
- Premium pharmacy: Rs 1,300–1,999 (La Roche-Posay top, Apivita)
- Mineral budget online: Rs 375–425 (Maxiblock)

* Reef-safe mineral formulation cost vs. retail price acceptance — to be tested in Phase 3.
* Tropical-climate stability of formulation and packaging — to be validated in Phase 2.
* La Roche-Posay has independent marine safety testing (Tara Pacific) — Tec-Tec will need a credible reef-safe substantiation strategy to stand alongside this.
