## 1. Nivea (Beiersdorf)

**Parent:** Beiersdorf AG, Germany.  
**Positioning:** Mass-market sun care leader. "Trusted protection for the whole family." Value-driven. Available everywhere.

**Hero Claims:**
- Protect & Moisture / Protect & Hydrate SPF50+ body lotion
- Reef-safe compliant (European range): free from oxybenzone, octinoxate, octocrylene, microplastics
- Water-resistant; moisturising; lightweight texture

**SPF Range:** SPF 15, 30, 50, 50+

**Chemistry:** Primarily **chemical** (organic filters). European range uses Tinosorb S, Tinosorb M, avobenzone, homosalate. Free of oxybenzone and octinoxate in Europe — compliant with Hawaii Reef Bill. A mineral/reef-safe sub-range exists (UK/Australia) but is not the hero SKU globally.

**Pack Sizes:** 125ml, 200ml, 300ml, 400ml (body lotion); 50ml (face). Pump and tube formats.

**Retail Price in Mauritius (MUR):**
- TBC — needs in-store check at Winners / Super U / Intermart.
- Reference: UK Superdrug price ~£7–9 for 200ml (≈ Rs 450–580 landed). Mauritius retail likely **Rs 280–550** range given mass-market supermarket positioning.

**Distribution Channels in Mauritius:**
- Supermarkets (Winners, Super U, Intermart, Jumbo) — confirmed broad presence
- Mass-market pharmacy and general retail
- *Not* stocked in specialist pharmacy (MedActiv catalogue does not list Nivea)

**Perceived Strengths:**
- Enormous brand recognition globally and in Mauritius; trusted by families
- Price leadership — most accessible SPF50 on the shelf
- Wide availability: near-universal supermarket distribution

**Perceived Weaknesses:**
- Chemical filters only in mass range — no mineral differentiation
- No cause narrative or local connection
- Generic, category-average positioning — nothing to anchor loyalty
- Reef-safe claim is compliance-driven, not a brand value

**Reef-Safe Credentials:** Partially. European-formulated products are oxybenzone/octinoxate-free and comply with Hawaii Reef Bill. No dedicated reef-safe positioning; no independent marine testing. Claim is "compliant" rather than "proven." Nano-particle status unclear.

**Retail Price in Mauritius (MUR):**

- In-store supermarket price TBC — needs in-store check.

**Distribution Channels in Mauritius:**
- Supermarkets (Winners, Super U, Intermart) — strong mass-market presence

**Perceived Strengths:**

**Perceived Weaknesses:**

## 5. Bioderma (NAOS Group)

**Pack Sizes:** 40ml (face), 50ml (face), 75ml (mineral fluid), 200ml (body spray/lotion).

**Retail Price in Mauritius (MUR):**
- TBC — needs in-store pharmacy check. Not found in MedActiv online catalogue.

**Perceived Strengths:**
- Trusted by dermatologists for sensitive, atopic, and reactive skin

**Perceived Weaknesses:**

## 6. Stream2Sea

**Positioning:** "Consciously Made for YOU and Our Blue Planet." Mission-first. The marine biodegradability and reef-safety pioneer. Founder-led activist brand.

- Only personal care brand with products independently tested and proven safe for freshwater fish, saltwater fish, and coral larvae
- Zero oxybenzone, octinoxate, parabens, nanoparticles, synthetic fragrance
- Biodegradable formulas; eco-packaging
- Part proceeds to ocean conservation

**SPF Range:** SPF 20, 30, 40

**Chemistry:** 100% **mineral** (titanium dioxide; some SKUs with zinc oxide). Non-nano. Biodegradable botanicals (green tea, tulsi, wakame, olive leaf). No chemical UV filters.

**Pack Sizes:** 3 fl oz (89ml) — primary format; also 1 fl oz travel 3-pack; tin formats.

**Retail Price in Mauritius (MUR):**
- **Not sold in Mauritius retail.** Import only via international platforms (Amazon US, brand website).
- US retail price: ~$18–28 per 3oz bottle (≈ Rs 900–1,400 before shipping/duties).

**Distribution Channels in Mauritius:**
- None confirmed. Online import only. Not present in any Mauritius retail channel found.

**Perceived Strengths:**
- Strongest marine safety credentials in the market — independently certified, published scientific data
- Authentic founder story with credibility (cosmetic chemist + diver who witnessed the problem)
- Mission-led with genuine cause contribution
- Non-nano mineral formulation — the cleanest reef-safe claim on the market

**Perceived Weaknesses:**
- SPF maxes at 40 — no SPF50+ option (limitation for tropical markets)
- Not sold in Mauritius — import friction means it is not a competitive threat on shelf
- White cast can be significant (titanium dioxide dominant formula)
- Primarily US/dive tourism market — limited consumer recognition in Indian Ocean region
- Small format (89ml) may feel expensive for a family beach day

**Reef-Safe Credentials:** Excellent — the most rigorous in the competitive set. Marine biodegradability tested independently. Non-nano mineral only. No contested ingredients.

## 10. White Space Synthesis for Tec-Tec Cream

*Where does genuine unoccupied space exist for a reef-safe mineral + cause-led + Mauritian-made brand?*

### Finding 1: No Mauritius-Origin Sunscreen Exists

There is no sunscreen brand in Mauritius — or in the Indian Ocean region — that is (a) made in or for Mauritius, (b) carries a local cause narrative, and (c) is mineral/reef-safe. Every brand on shelf is either European (La Roche-Posay, Bioderma, Eucerin, Garnier, Nivea, EQ) or from the Anglosphere (Thinksport, Stream2Sea, Maxiblock) or South Africa (Ocean Freedom, Pradiance). The **"made for Mauritius"** space is entirely vacant.

### Finding 2: Reef-Safe Mineral Sunscreen Has Pharmacy Shelf Space in Mauritius But Is Under-Branded

Pradiance and Maxiblock confirm that mineral sunscreen can reach Mauritian shelves (pharmacy and eco-online channels), but neither has meaningful brand presence, local story, or cause positioning. The mineral format is not yet "owned" by any brand in the Mauritius pharmacy channel the way La Roche-Posay owns the clinical/sensitive segment.

### Finding 3: The Rs 800–1,400 Pharmacy Price Band Is Occupied by Chemical Brands With No Cause Story

La Roche-Posay dominates the Rs 820–1,550 band with clinical credibility but zero cause narrative and zero local identity. A mineral, cause-led, local brand at Rs 900–1,300 for the Family SKU can sit alongside (or slightly below) La Roche-Posay in the pharmacy channel and offer something La Roche-Posay cannot: a story, a donation, and a reef-safe commitment that is a brand pillar rather than a compliance footnote.

### Finding 4: Cause-Led Sunscreen Does Not Exist in the Market

Stream2Sea is the closest global analog — mission-first, marine-tested, cause contribution built in. But it does not sell in Mauritius, it has a maximum SPF of 40, and its cause is ocean-generic (not local). **No competitor brand in this market links sunscreen purchase directly to a named Mauritius skin cancer cause.** This is the sharpest white space.

### Finding 5: The Tourist Channel Is Entirely Unoccupied by Reef-Safe Mineral Brands

Hotel gift shops, boat operators (Blue Safari), excursion desks: none of these currently carry a Mauritius-origin, reef-safe, reef-protection-framed sunscreen. The travel retail and boat distribution channel has no incumbent. Tec-Tec's first distribution point (Blue Safari boats) puts it into a channel where no competitor currently plays.

### Finding 6: The "Reef-Safe" Claim Is a Mess in the Market

La Roche-Posay has the best reef-safe science but doesn't lead with it. Nivea and Garnier claim compliance but use octocrylene and chemical filters. Bioderma's mineral range uses nano-particles. EQ and Stream2Sea have genuine credentials but zero Mauritius shelf presence. The market has reef-safe confusion — which is an opportunity to own the claim authentically with a clear, simple, non-nano mineral formula and on-pack transparency.
